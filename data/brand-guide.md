# Aroma Cloud — Brand & Marketing Guide

This document is the source of truth for how Aroma Cloud shows up in marketing.
Every campaign generated by `/api/marketing-campaign` uses this file (alongside
`company-overview.md`) as persistent brand context, so consistency lives here —
not in any one campaign brief.

---

## Brand essence

- **One-line positioning:** Single-origin coffee, traceable to the farm,
  roasted to your taste profile, in your hands within 72 hours of roast.
- **What we are:** A specialty roaster that treats subscribers like the
  beverage program of a great cafe — opinionated, transparent, never generic.
- **What we are not:** A "convenience" brand. Not Nespresso. Not a daily-deal
  coffee site. Not a lifestyle prop.

---

## Voice and tone

| Attribute | Yes | No |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Tone | Warm, curious, lightly nerdy | Slick, salesy, hype-y |
| Vocabulary | Crop year, varietal, processing method, roast curve | "Game-changing", "elevate", "unlock" |
| Energy | Calm enthusiasm | Caffeinated yelling |
| Humor | Self-aware, dry, occasional coffee-snob jokes at our own expense | Irony at the expense of the grower |
| Formatting | Short paragraphs, generous whitespace, occasional bullet | Wall of text, exclamation points |

Sentence patterns we like:
- *"This one's a Geisha from Volcán Barú — bright bergamot, peach skin, finishes clean."*
- *"We over-shipped the Yirgacheffe natural last month. Subscribers loved it. We're bringing it back as a single-week drop."*

Phrases we will not ship:
- "Liquid energy"
- "Fuel your hustle"
- "Coffee-lover's dream"
- Any pun involving "brew-tiful", "espress-yourself", or "a latte love"

## Audience

Three personas in priority order. Campaigns should always have at least one in mind.

1. **Sophie, 31, Brooklyn — "the upgrader"** *(highest LTV)*
   Has a Fellow Stagg, a burr grinder, watches James Hoffmann on YouTube. Was
   a Trade subscriber for two years before switching to us. Cares about origin
   stories, processing methods, and consistency. Will pay $24/bag.

2. **Marcus, 44, Atlanta — "the routinist"** *(highest retention)*
   French press every morning, same beans every two weeks. Doesn't want to
   *think* about coffee, but wants it to be obviously better than grocery
   store. Subscribed after a wedding gift. Cares about: it shows up on time,
   it tastes good, the bag isn't confusing.

3. **Aria, 26, Los Angeles — "the discoverer"** *(highest social reach)*
   Posts pour-over videos on TikTok. Tries every new roaster. Will switch
   away after 2 months but will tag us 8 times before they go. Treat this
   audience as a top-of-funnel awareness play, not LTV.

Audiences we do **not** chase:
- Office bulk buyers (margins don't work, brand dilution)
- Cold-brew bottled-beverage drinkers (different shopping mindset)
- Anyone primarily motivated by caffeine kick (wrong product story)

## Channel preferences

| Channel | Use it for | Don't use it for |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| TikTok Shop | Discovery + Aria-persona acquisition | Hard direct-response |
| Instagram (organic) | Single-origin "drop" announcements, sourcing trip footage | Daily posts |
| Email | Subscriber-only previews, profile updates, restock notices | Cold acquisition |
| YouTube (long-form) | Origin trip mini-docs, brewing technique with our beans | Ad-driven shorts |
| Podcasts (sponsorship) | Tech / design / cooking shows with Sophie-shaped audiences | Hustle / business shows |

We have **paused** paid Meta acquisition since Q3 2025 — CAC was $61 against
a $32 AOV. Do not propose Meta paid in campaigns unless explicitly asked.

## Creator collaboration rules

- **Yes:** Coffee educators, single-roaster reviewers, slow-living lifestyle
  creators, food/cooking creators with a clear point of view, bookish or
  design-leaning lifestyle accounts.
- **No:** Generic "morning routine" influencers, anything that requires us
  to ship 50 bags as a giveaway, anyone whose primary content is dance or
  prank, anyone who has previously promoted instant-coffee competitors in
  the past six months.
- **Briefing convention:** We send creators *one bag of two different
  origins* and ask them to record a side-by-side. We do not script the
  content. We do require them to disclose the partnership in the first 3
  seconds.
- **Compensation:** Flat fee + 15% rev-share on attributed first-month
  subscriptions for 90 days. We never ask for "exposure" trades.

## Visual identity (for reference in concept-level briefs)

- **Palette:** Off-white (#F4EFE6), espresso brown (#3B2A20), single accent
  per campaign (often a copper or muted terracotta).
- **Type:** Editorial serif for headlines (we use *Tiempos Headline*), grotesk
  for body. No script fonts. No all-caps headlines longer than three words.
- **Photography:** Natural light, real beans, real cups. We do not use stock.
  Hands and tools welcome; faces optional. Avoid "clean girl" aesthetics —
  we lean closer to *Cereal Magazine* than *Glossier*.

## Past-campaign signatures (what's worked, what to avoid)

The campaign orchestrator also reads compressed `## Memory note` paragraphs
from past runs in `data/campaigns/`. The principles below are stable across
runs and should override any conflicting pattern in those memory notes.

**Worked:**
- "Single-week drops" (limited-quantity, named lots) — drives 4–6× normal
  email open rates and ~30% conversion to add-on purchases.
- Origin trip footage shot on phone, edited tight (45–75s) — outperforms
  studio video at every funnel step we measure.
- Letting the grower speak. Always more interesting than us speaking.

**Did not work — do not repeat without a strong reason:**
- "Brew-along" livestreams (Sept 2024). Low attendance, awkward energy.
- Holiday gift bundles styled like luxury chocolate boxes (Nov 2024). Felt
  off-voice; subscribers wrote in to say so.
- Any campaign anchored on caffeine content / energy claims.
- Discount-stacked first-month promos > 25% off — attracts churners.

## Compliance and brand-safety checklist (for every campaign)

A campaign passes review only if:

1. The grower / origin is named or visible somewhere in the creative.
2. No health or energy claims.
3. No promo deeper than 25% off the first bag, or 1 free bag with a 3-month
   commitment.
4. Any creator partnership uses our standard disclosure template.
5. The campaign's `## Memory note` paragraph (saved automatically) actually
   captures the *distinctive* choice — not the brief verbatim — so the next
   campaign can avoid repeating it.

## Glossary (terms a campaign can use without defining)

- **Profile-confirmed:** A subscriber has rated their last 3 bags so the
  algorithm knows their roast preference.
- **Lot:** A specific harvest from a specific farm/cooperative — distinct
  from "blend".
- **Drop:** A small-quantity release (typically 200–500 bags) of a single
  lot, available for ~7 days or until it sells out.
- **House profile:** Our default roast curve for subscribers who haven't
  customized — slightly under-developed, optimized for filter brewing.
